ErgoDox

Pan-pan, half split ortholinear keyboard.

My first 'custom' mechanical keyboard, of course it had to be outragous compared with all the ones I've owned previously, which were just plain, plasic black slabs.
View Keyboard

SPACE65:CyberVoyager

A premium 65% keeb featuring a chonky two-tone aluminium design.

My first notion of an 'endgame' keyboard (yeah right). I appreciate the 65% layout with dedicated arrow keys and a few extra buttons to serve as other function keys.
View Keyboard
The Rest

Now for the rest of the crew! I wanted to explore a few different layouts and along with that different switches.

Ajazz K620T v2

A 60% bluetooth, hotswap mechanical keyboard.

I purchased this as I had been looking for a wireless keybaord and decided to go with a mechanical one because why not. This is my first hotswap board which will be good for when I get sick of the linear switches it comes with.

It also has a slot that will fit a tablet in there up to 11".

NIU45

A teeny-tiny 40% board.

This keyboard was supposed to be the one that you would take around, or take to work and use. I got used to it pretty quickly and once you learn the ropes and train your fingers, you end up being quite efficient with it.

I love things that are acrylic or to any degree, transparent.

GK64

My first custom keyboard (which was a pre-built).

This was during a time where I was looking at keyboards on AliExpress, and I thought this one would be awesome, something less than TKL (ten keyless) and Type-C.

This was a time where I got a nice set of SA keycaps as well, which I don't enojoy typing on anymore.

Sesame

A through-hole Alice-type keybaord.

I purchased this kit to have a look at an Alice-type keyboard as I was interested in the more ergonomic layout, whilst having it all on one piece of PCB.
